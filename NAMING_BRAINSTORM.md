# Naming & Logo Brainstorm

> "TaskDog" is a placeholder name. The real name should be short, memorable, and convey the outcome: a life organized from WhatsApp.

---

## Naming Principles

| Principle | Why |
|---|---|
| **One word** | App store search, favicon, and verbal recall all favor short names |
| **No "AI" suffix** | Dated, every competitor does it. Think Anthropic, Linear, Notion |
| **No "Task"** | Too generic. The product is bigger than task management |
| **Easy to spell** | Users need to type it, search it, say it |
| **Aspirational** | The name should describe the outcome (clarity, order, peace) |
| **Available .com or simple TLD** | Must pass the domain check |

## Personal Recommendation

**Top pick: Clarity**

- It communicates the fundamental outcome — not "I managed my tasks" but "I have clarity on what matters"
- One word, easy to spell, works in any language
- Strong .com potential (clarity.app, clarity.so, clarity.ai)
- Works with the minimalist design language already built
- Universal appeal (not techy, not corporate, not cutesy)
- Pairs naturally with a tagline like "Clarity from conversation" or "Your life, organized"

**Runner-up: Prism**

- More distinctive than Clarity (fewer competitors with that name)
- The metaphor is beautiful and accurate — chats go in, organized tasks come out
- Naturally suggests a colorful, vibrant brand identity
- Tagline: "Find your spectrum" or "Light from noise"

## Logo Ideas

### Visual direction

The logo should be **hyper-minimal**, work at 16px (app icon), and convey **order emerging from conversation**.

### Concept 1: The Ordered Dot

```
  ⬤       A single chat bubble / dot
  │       with organized lines radiating from it
  │─ ─ ─
  │       Minimalist. Suggests "one conversation → many organized items"
```

A filled circle (the chat/message) with 3–4 clean horizontal lines extending to the right (the organized output). Like a single seed growing into structured rows.

**Style:** Mono-weight line icon. Works in both filled and outline variants.

### Concept 2: The Fold

```
  ┌─┐     A simple geometric shape
  │ │     that suggests something being folded into place
  └─┘     or slotted neatly
```

A square folded diagonally, or a card sliding into a slot. Suggests: "everything has its place. organized."

**Style:** Bold geometric. One or two colors.

### Concept 3: The Check Bubble

```
  (✓)    A chat bubble outline
         with a single checkmark inside
```

Simple. Instantly understood. The checkmark communicates completion, organization, clarity. The bubble says "this came from conversation."

**Style:** Thick outline, bold check. Saturated background color.

### Concept 4: The Prism

```
  △      A triangle (prism)
  │      with one line entering from left
  │││    and multiple organized lines exiting right
```

A literal prism. Chaos in → order out. Abstract enough to be distinctive, concrete enough to be understood.

**Style:** Geometric, 2-3 colors, works as a gradient.

### Concept 5: Pure Typemark

```
  C      Just the letter "C" (or "L" or "S")
         in a distinctive, custom geometric style
         on a colored background
```

Follows the Notion/Linear/Things approach. A single letter in a bespoke typeface. Extremely scalable, works everywhere.

**Style:** Bold, geometric, distinctive stroke details.

## App Icon Direction

Based on 2026 ASO research:
- **Saturated color background** (not white — disappears in iOS grid)
- **Minimalist mark** centered, 90% of icon frame
- **One focal point** — the mark, not a combination of elements
- **High contrast** with competitors (if they're blue, go purple/orange/emerald)

The current TaskDog purple (`#7C3AED`) is actually a strong, distinctive color that stands out from the sea of blue productivity apps. Worth keeping or evolving into the new brand.
